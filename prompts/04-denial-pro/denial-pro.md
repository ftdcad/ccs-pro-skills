# CCS Denial Pro™ — Coverage Denial Fighter

## Overview

Reads the carrier's denial letter, isolates their ONE stated reason, and builds CCS's
rebuttal position around that exact reason — nothing more.

**Three Inputs:**
1. **Denial Letter** (required — the carrier's actual written denial)
2. **Complete Policy** (required — with all exclusions, endorsements, and riders)
3. **Loss Photos** (required — documentation of the damage being denied)

**Chain context (pull from prior PRO reports):**
- Strategy Pro report — gap analysis and leverage points already identified
- Scope Pro report — damage documentation and line items

**Output:** A CCS-branded `.docx` Denial Analysis Report with Mr. Hyde / Dr. Jekyll
analysis, CCS position statement, evidence checklist, and next actions.

**Next step after this PRO:** State PRO (not Undisputed Funds — state statutes and
deadlines must be confirmed before any correspondence is generated).

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE — Fight What They Actually Said

**One denial letter. One stated reason. One rebuttal.**

- Read the denial letter. Find their ONE specific stated reason. Quote it exactly.
- Do not build arguments against reasons they didn't give.
- Do not scatter the rebuttal across multiple theories — one argument, the strongest one.
- Every rebuttal must anchor to specific policy language, not general principle.
- If coverage was extended to any claimed item — even partial payment — this is the
  wrong track. Route back to Strategy PRO for Loss Below assignment.

---

## ROUTING CHECK — Run Before Any Analysis

> **Did the carrier extend ANY coverage to ANY claimed item — even partial payment?**

- **YES** → Stop. Route back to Strategy PRO. Partial payment = Loss Below, not Denial.
- **NO** → Full coverage denial confirmed. Proceed.

If the letter is ambiguous — flag it in Agentic Notes and ask the PA to confirm before proceeding.

---

## Step 1 — Extract the ONE Denial Reason

Read the full denial letter. Carriers pad denials with boilerplate and general policy
language. Cut through it. Find the ONE specific reason they are denying this specific claim.

**Common denial reason categories:**
- **No covered peril** — carrier claims no qualifying storm event on the date of loss
- **Pre-existing / maintenance** — damage predates the loss or is wear and tear
- **Cosmetic exclusion** — damage is cosmetic only, not functional
- **Cause of loss exclusion** — flood, earth movement, settling, or other excluded cause
- **Late notice** — insured or CCS did not report within the required timeframe
- **Vacancy / occupancy** — property was vacant or unoccupied at time of loss
- **ACV roof endorsement** — scheduled depreciation reduced payment to zero (note: if
  carrier paid something under ACV, this may be Loss Below, not Denial — verify)
- **Prior damage not repaired** — prior loss was not remediated before new loss occurred
- **Policy not in force** — coverage lapsed, non-payment, or cancellation

**Quote the denial reason exactly as written.** Do not paraphrase. The exact language
matters — the rebuttal lives or dies on what the letter actually says.

---

## Step 2 — Mr. Hyde Analysis (Carrier's Position)

Present the carrier's argument as they would make it — completely and fairly.
Understanding their position is the only way to destroy it.

- **Their ONE stated reason** — exact quote from the denial letter
- **Policy section cited** — section number, page, endorsement reference if specified
- **Their logic** — how they connect the policy language to the denial conclusion
- **Evidence they relied on** — inspection report, photos, weather data, field notes, or none stated

A weak Mr. Hyde section produces a weak rebuttal. Do not shortchange this step.

---

## Step 3 — Dr. Jekyll Rebuttal (CCS Counter-Position)

One argument. The strongest one. Anchored to policy language and evidence.

- **Policy interpretation** — why coverage applies; quote the specific policy language
  that supports coverage — not just the general coverage grant, but the specific language
  that defeats their stated exclusion or denial basis
- **Their mistake** — the exact flaw in the carrier's reasoning:
  - Misapplied exclusion (exclusion doesn't say what they claim it says)
  - Wrong policy section cited
  - Evidence that directly contradicts their position (photos, storm data, inspection report)
  - Ambiguous language that must be construed in favor of the insured
  - Endorsement that conflicts with or supersedes the section they cited
- **Winning argument** — one sentence. The clearest possible statement of why coverage applies.

---

## Step 4 — Evidence Checklist

What does CCS need to prove the rebuttal? Be specific. Generic lists are useless.
Build this checklist based on the denial reason identified in Step 1.

**For No Covered Peril denials:**
- [ ] Storm verification data — NOAA, CoreLogic Hail, WeatherFusion, or equivalent for exact date and location
- [ ] Neighboring property claims or carrier payments for same event (if obtainable)
- [ ] Photos with metadata confirming post-storm condition
- [ ] Contractor or PA field notes dated proximate to loss

**For Pre-existing / Maintenance denials:**
- [ ] Pre-loss photos (Google Street View historical, prior inspection reports, real estate listing photos)
- [ ] Contractor affidavit distinguishing storm damage from wear
- [ ] Prior carrier inspections that did not note the condition being claimed as pre-existing
- [ ] Permit history showing prior repairs were completed

**For Cosmetic Exclusion denials:**
- [ ] Policy language — does the exclusion apply to the specific component being denied?
- [ ] Functional damage documentation — does the damage affect the component's ability to perform its intended function?
- [ ] Manufacturer specifications — does the damage void warranty or require replacement per manufacturer?
- [ ] Photos showing functional impairment beyond cosmetic impact

**For Cause of Loss Exclusion denials:**
- [ ] Policy ensuing loss language — does storm/wind creating an opening override the exclusion?
- [ ] Expert report or contractor affidavit on actual cause of loss
- [ ] Photos showing storm entry point vs. claimed excluded cause

**For Late Notice denials:**
- [ ] Date CCS was retained
- [ ] Date claim was filed with carrier
- [ ] Policy's notice requirement — exact language and deadline
- [ ] Any carrier waiver of notice (prior communications, acknowledgment letters)
- [ ] State law prejudice requirement — most states require carrier to show actual prejudice

**For Vacancy / Occupancy denials:**
- [ ] Policy's vacancy definition — exact language and day threshold
- [ ] Evidence of occupancy (utility records, mail, personal property photos, neighbor statements)
- [ ] Date vacancy began if applicable
- [ ] Whether the specific peril is excluded under the vacancy clause or just reduced

**For Prior Damage denials:**
- [ ] Prior claim documentation
- [ ] Repair records, permits, or contractor invoices showing prior damage was remediated
- [ ] Photos distinguishing prior damage from current loss
- [ ] Timeline of prior loss vs. current loss

---

## Step 5 — PA Action Plan

Numbered, specific, executable. No vague steps.

1. **Peer review** — mandatory on all denial track files. Submit to CRM within 24 hours.
   Come prepared with assessment of strengths, weaknesses, and proposed solution.
2. **Evidence acquisition** — identify missing items from the Evidence Checklist.
   Assign responsibility and deadline for each.
3. **Reinspection demand** — determine whether a joint reinspection with a CCS expert
   is the right next move before any written response to the carrier.
4. **Coverage counsel flag** — if the denial basis involves complex policy interpretation,
   endorsement conflict, or bad faith indicators, flag for coverage counsel before responding.
5. **State PRO** — before any correspondence is drafted, proceed to State PRO to confirm
   the applicable statutes, deadlines, and carrier obligations for this state.

### AGENTIC NOTES

Observation-only flags. This section is the loaded brief that downstream PROs
— including State PRO and all correspondence PROs — draw from.

**Always check and include if applicable:**

- **Bad faith indicators** — denial without inspection, denial contradicts carrier's own
  prior communications, unreasonable or unsupported denial basis
- **Coverage counsel triggers** — policy ambiguity, conflicting endorsements, novel
  exclusion application that warrants legal review
- **Statute of limitations exposure** — if the denial letter starts any clock running,
  flag the date and the resulting deadline explicitly
- **Appraisal clause availability** — some policies allow appraisal even on a denial;
  note whether the clause is available and quote the relevant language
- **Carrier pattern** — if this is a known carrier tactic on a known denial type, note it
- **Policy Pro cross-flags** — endorsements or exclusions flagged in Policy Pro that
  are directly relevant to this denial basis
- **Scope Pro cross-flags** — damage items in the scope that the carrier's denial
  does not address (items ignored vs. items denied — those are different problems)
- **Reinspection recommendation** — if denial was issued without a carrier inspection
  or with a clearly inadequate one, flag explicitly
- **Time-sensitive evidence** — storm data windows close; photo metadata can be lost;
  contractors move on. Flag anything that must be obtained immediately.

**If nothing notable:** Write "No additional observations."

## Edge Cases

- **Denial letter cites multiple reasons** — identify the PRIMARY reason (most prominently
  stated or most likely to succeed for carrier). Note secondary reasons in Agentic Notes.
- **No policy provided** — invoke Policy Pro Override protocol. Stamp the document. Do not
  guess at policy language.
- **Denial issued without any carrier inspection** — flag as potential bad faith in Agentic
  Notes. Note in Evidence Checklist that no inspection was conducted.
- **Carrier cites a policy section that doesn't exist or is mis-cited** — document it
  carefully. A mis-cited section is a significant rebuttal asset.
- **Denial arrives within days of claim submission** — rapid denial without investigation
  is a bad faith indicator in most states. Flag for peer review immediately.
- **Carrier denies and simultaneously invokes appraisal** — contradictory in most states.
  Flag for coverage counsel. Note in Agentic Notes.
- **Photos are unavailable or inadequate** — note what is missing and add to Evidence
  Checklist as first priority. Do not fabricate or assume.

## What This Skill Does NOT Do

- Does not draft carrier correspondence, demand letters, or rebuttal letters
  (that is State PRO → downstream correspondence PROs)
- Does not calculate claim value or settlement amounts
- Does not make coverage determinations — it analyzes the carrier's position and
  builds the factual and policy basis for the PA to act on
- Does not replace peer review — it triggers it
- Does not research state-specific statutes or deadlines — that is State PRO
