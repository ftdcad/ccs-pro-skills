# CCS New Claim Pro™ — Early Claim Positioning & Forward Pressure

## Overview

Gets CCS's position documented and submitted before the carrier's adjuster sets the
settlement baseline — and keeps the file moving forward at every stage.

**Three Inputs:**
1. **Completed Scope Pro Report + Xactimate Estimate** (required — CCS's full position)
2. **Policy Pro Report** (required — coverage, deductibles, endorsements)
3. **Current Carrier Communications** (required — acknowledgment letter, claim number,
   or confirmation of current status)

**Output:** A CCS-branded .docx New Claim Positioning Report with submission strategy,
coverage issue proactive response, homeowner field prep, forward pressure action plan,
and anticipated carrier response scenario.

**Next step after this PRO:** State PRO — statutes and timelines must be confirmed
before any formal correspondence is generated.

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE — Move First. Never Wait Passively.

**The carrier's first adjuster inspection sets the anchor for every negotiation that follows.
CCS must establish its position before that anchor is set.**

- Submit the CCS scope before or concurrent with the carrier's inspection — never after
- Passive waiting is never acceptable — carrier delay tactics benefit from inaction
- Demand undisputed funds early — do not let the carrier sit on any portion of the
  claim that is not in genuine dispute
- Forward pressure at every stage: if the carrier has not responded, CCS acts; if the
  carrier has responded, CCS advances; the file never stalls
- A complete early submission beats a complete late submission every time

---

## ROUTING CHECK — Confirm This Is the Right Track

> **1. Has the carrier issued a formal written denial of any claimed item?**
- **YES** → Route to Denial PRO.

> **2. Has the carrier issued a payment or estimate below CCS's scope?**
- **YES** → Route to Loss Below PRO.

> **3. Is the claim acknowledged (or newly submitted) with no payment or denial yet?**
- **YES** → New Claim track confirmed. Proceed.

## Step 1 — Establish CCS's Opening Position

```
CCS Estimate (RCV):           $__________
Applicable Deductible:        $__________
CCS Net Claim Value:          $__________
Peril:                        [Wind / Hail / Water / Other]
Date of Loss:                 __________
Claim Number:                 __________
Carrier:                      __________
Carrier Adjuster (if assigned): __________
Carrier Inspection Date (if scheduled): __________
```

**Coverage concerns from Policy Pro — flag proactively:**
- ACV roof endorsement (will depreciation significantly reduce initial payment?)
- Matching exclusion (carrier may limit scope to damaged sections only)
- Cosmetic exclusion (carrier may challenge functional damage)
- High or percentage deductible (calculate actual dollar impact)
- Late notice exposure (confirm filing date vs. policy requirement)

These are not problems yet. Knowing them now shapes how CCS frames the submission
and what documentation to gather before the carrier's inspection.

---

## Step 2 — Early Submission Strategy

**The goal: CCS scope on file before the carrier's adjuster arrives.**

**Submission readiness checklist:**
- [ ] Scope Pro report finalized (no revisions pending)
- [ ] Xactimate estimate finalized
- [ ] Supporting photos organized and labeled
- [ ] Carrier claim number confirmed
- [ ] Submission method confirmed (carrier portal, email, certified mail)

**If estimate has pending revisions** — do not submit until clean. One clean submission
beats a revised submission every time for credibility.

**If carrier inspection is imminent (within 48-72 hours)** — expedite. An incomplete
submission before inspection beats a complete submission after it.

**If carrier has not yet scheduled an inspection** — submit CCS scope immediately.
Do not wait for their scheduling process.

---

## Step 3 — Forward Pressure Framework

**CCS never waits passively. Every stage has an active next move.**

### If carrier has not yet responded:
- Submit CCS scope (Step 2)
- Send written status request — document that CCS is actively engaged
- Note the carrier's acknowledgment deadline for State PRO (varies by state)
- Prepare for their response based on the anticipated scenario (Step 5)

### If carrier has acknowledged but not inspected:
- Confirm inspection date in writing
- Brief homeowner (Step 4)
- Prepare supplement list for any items likely to be missed

### If carrier has inspected but not issued estimate:
- Follow up in writing — document the follow-up date
- Note elapsed time since inspection
- Flag for State PRO: most states have carrier response deadlines post-inspection

### If carrier has issued an ACV payment only (depreciation withheld, no denial):
- This is still New Claim track — depreciation recovery is forward pressure
- Document receipt of ACV payment
- Prepare depreciation release demand for when repairs are underway or complete
- Do not confuse ACV payment with settlement — the file is not closed

**Demand undisputed funds at every opportunity.** If any portion of the claim is not
genuinely disputed by the carrier, CCS should be pressing for that payment to move.
Carrier delay tactics lose their leverage when undisputed funds are already released.

## Step 4 — Homeowner Field Preparation

**Every New Claim report includes homeowner field instructions. No exceptions.**

The PA delivers these to the homeowner before the carrier's inspection:

1. **The carrier's inspector has zero financial authority.** They cannot approve,
   deny, or settle the claim on site. Nothing they say during the inspection is
   a settlement offer or a final determination.

2. **CCS will not be present during the carrier's inspection.** This is standard
   practice. The carrier's adjuster works faster and documents more thoroughly
   when CCS is not present. Their unassisted process often creates a record
   that benefits CCS.

3. **Answer questions honestly and briefly.** The homeowner does not need to
   volunteer information beyond what is directly asked. "I noticed damage after
   the storm" is a complete answer.

4. **Get the inspector's name, company, and contact information** before they leave.

5. **Note the inspection date and approximate duration.** This matters if the
   carrier later claims the inspection was thorough.

6. **Do not allow access to areas outside the scope of the claim.** If the claim
   is for storm damage to the roof and interior, the inspector's access is limited
   to those areas.

---

## Step 5 — Anticipated Carrier Response

Based on the carrier, scope, and Policy Pro flags — identify the most likely first
response. This determines what State PRO and downstream PROs need ready.

**Scenario A — Carrier agrees with most of CCS's scope (low dispute risk)**
Indicators: Clean policy, no problematic endorsements, well-documented storm,
responsive carrier on this loss type.
Likely outcome: Payment near CCS scope with depreciation withheld.
Preparation: Clean submission, full documentation, depreciation release demand ready.

**Scenario B — Carrier underpays significantly (high Loss Below risk)**
Indicators: Known underpaying carrier, ACV endorsement on roof, complex scope
with O&P and matching exposure, interior ensuing loss.
Preparation: Submit with O&P justification upfront, document matching argument
preemptively, prepare supplement before carrier estimate even arrives.

**Scenario C — Carrier moves toward denial (moderate denial risk)**
Indicators: Older property, pre-existing flags in Policy Pro, marginal storm event
data, late notice exposure, carrier with aggressive denial history on this loss type.
Preparation: Storm verification data and pre-loss documentation before inspection.
Have reinspection demand language ready. Peer review before carrier responds.

Note the scenario in the report. If Scenario B or C — flag in Agentic Notes with
specific preparation items for the anticipated track shift.

---

## Step 6 — PA Action Plan

1. **Submission** — submit CCS scope and estimate to carrier via confirmed method.
   Record submission date, method, and confirmation number in claim file immediately.
2. **Homeowner prep** — communicate field instructions before carrier inspection.
3. **Estimate status** — confirm finalized before submission. No pending revisions.
4. **Undisputed funds demand** — identify any portion of the claim the carrier has
   no legitimate basis to dispute. Press for release of those funds first.
5. **Inspection monitoring** — confirm carrier inspection date. Document in file.
6. **Peer review** — required if claim value $100K+, Scenario C anticipated,
   significant coverage concerns from Policy Pro, or new adjuster on file.
7. **State PRO** — before any formal correspondence is generated, proceed to State PRO
   to confirm submission requirements, carrier response deadlines, and filing obligations.

## Document Structure

**Title:** [Claim #] | [Insured Name] | New Claim Pro™
Prepared by CCS New Claim Pro™ | [Date]

### CCS OPENING POSITION
Summary table — RCV, deductible, net claim value, peril, date of loss.
Coverage concern flags (proactive — from Policy Pro).

### EARLY SUBMISSION STRATEGY
Checklist status. Recommended method and timing.

### FORWARD PRESSURE FRAMEWORK
Current carrier status. Active next move for this specific stage.
Undisputed funds identification.

### HOMEOWNER FIELD PREPARATION
Numbered talking points. Present in every report — every time.

### ANTICIPATED CARRIER RESPONSE
Scenario A, B, or C with indicators and preparation note.

### CRITICAL ACTIONS
Numbered from Step 6. Peer review is Item 1 if triggered.

### NEXT STEP
Proceed to State PRO. Documents to bring listed.

### AGENTIC NOTES

Loaded brief for State PRO and downstream PROs. New Claim files can shift to
Denial or Loss Below — Agentic Notes front-load what the next PRO will need
if the track changes.

Check and include if applicable:
- **Track shift risk** — based on carrier and Policy Pro flags, which direction
  is this file likely to move? What are the triggers? Name the scenario.
- **Undisputed funds opportunity** — is any portion of this claim clearly owed
  regardless of the dispute? Flag it explicitly so forward pressure can be applied.
- **Storm data status** — has storm verification been obtained? For marginal events,
  flag now before the carrier's inspector creates a conflicting record.
- **Pre-existing exposure** — visible wear, prior damage history, or roof age that
  could support a pre-existing argument. Document what CCS has; flag what is missing.
- **Scope completeness** — components present on the property not included in the
  scope (second structures, HVAC, interior, screen enclosures). Flag as supplement
  candidates before carrier estimate creates a baseline.
- **Carrier reputation** — known behavior on this peril or property type. Anticipated
  response scenario should reflect it.
- **Inspection timing risk** — if carrier inspection is imminent and submission is
  not yet made, flag as urgent. Time is the most critical variable at this stage.
- **Policy Pro cross-flags** — any endorsement or provision requiring proactive action
  before the carrier inspection closes the window.

If nothing notable: "No additional observations."

## Output Rules

- Every report is a .docx file. No exceptions.
- Forward Pressure Framework is required — never omit.
- Homeowner field instructions are required in every report — never omit.
- CCS opening position must show net claim value (RCV minus deductible).
- Anticipated response scenario must be named — A, B, or C — with reasoning.
- Next Step directs to State PRO only.
- No submission cover letters or correspondence templates — that is downstream.
- Passive waiting is never a recommended action. If the PA has nothing to do,
  the report is incomplete.

## Edge Cases

- **Claim not yet filed with carrier** — filing with carrier is the first Critical Action.
  Note the filing date as pending.
- **Carrier has not acknowledged after 14+ days** — flag the inactivity. Document timeline.
  State PRO will confirm the acknowledgment deadline. This is not acceptable inaction —
  it is a forward pressure trigger.
- **Independent adjuster (IA) assigned instead of staff adjuster** — note it. IAs often
  move faster and have more latitude. Submission before IA inspection is even more
  critical than with staff adjusters.
- **Carrier assigns desk adjuster / no field inspection planned** — flag. Desk-only
  adjustments frequently underscope. Request field inspection in writing.
- **Referral source / contractor wants to attend carrier inspection** — advise against.
  The unassisted dynamic is a strategic asset. Document the conversation.
- **Multiple date-of-loss events on same property** — treat each separately. Note the
  relationship. Do not blend scope items from different events.
- **Estimate significantly higher than scope would suggest** — flag for estimate review
  before submission. An inflated estimate damages credibility on every line item.
- **ACV payment received but no denial issued** — still New Claim track. ACV payment
  is not settlement. Depreciation recovery is a forward pressure item. Document receipt.

## What This Skill Does NOT Do

- Does not draft submission cover letters, carrier correspondence, or homeowner letters
  (that is State PRO → downstream correspondence PROs)
- Does not build or modify Xactimate estimates — that is estimating
- Does not make coverage determinations — it positions the file and flags coverage risks
- Does not replace peer review — it triggers it when warranted
- Does not research state-specific filing requirements or deadlines — that is State PRO
