# Georgia -- State PRO Reference
**Source:** Merlin Law Group State Guide (Georgia Edition, for Licensed Public Adjusters)
**Last verified:** 2026-03-04

## 1. Notice of Loss

**Deadline:** Immediate.

- Notice of a claim is a **condition precedent** to recovery on a policy. *Lankford v. State Farm Mut. Auto. Ins. Co.*, 703 S.E.2d 436 (Ga. Ct. App. 2010).

## 2. Proof of Loss

**Deadline:** Look to policy provision -- typically 30 days, 60 days, or upon request.

- Proof of loss is a **condition precedent** to recovery on a policy. *Farmer v. Allstate Ins. Co.*, 396 F. Supp. 2d 1379 (N.D. Ga. 2005).
- **Carrier form-furnishing obligation:** The Proof of Loss form **shall be furnished by the insurer upon written request** to any person claiming to have a loss under the insurer's contract. **Failure or refusal to furnish the form upon written request or written notice of a loss shall constitute waiver** of the right of the insurer to require proof of loss. Ga. Code Ann. 33-24-39.

This is a significant defense tool: if the carrier does not provide a blank proof of loss form after receiving written request or notice of loss, the carrier waives the proof of loss requirement entirely.

## 3. Pre-Suit Notice Requirement + Cure Window

**60-day demand letter required before filing suit.**

- A demand letter must be sent to the insurer **60 days prior to filing suit** pursuant to Ga. Code Ann. 33-4-6.
- In an action brought pursuant to Ga. Code Ann. 33-4-6, and **within 20 days of bringing such action**, the insured shall -- in addition to service of process -- mail to the **Commissioner of Insurance** and the **consumers' insurance advocate** a copy of the demand and complaint by first-class mail. Ga. Code Ann. 33-4-6(b).

## 4. Carrier Acknowledgment Deadline

**15 days.**

- Insurers must **acknowledge communications within 15 days**. Ga. Comp. R. and Regs. 120-2-52.03.

## 5. Carrier Investigation / Payment Deadline

- Insurers must make a **prompt offer when liability is reasonably clear**. Ga. Code Ann. 33-6-34.
- Insurers must **accept or deny claims within a reasonable period of time**. Ga. Code Ann. 33-6-34.
- Insurers must **tender payment within 10 days after proof of loss**. Ga. Comp. R. and Regs. 120-2-52.03.

Note: Georgia does not have a rigid day-count framework like Texas's Prompt Payment Act or Florida's 90-day rule. The standard is "reasonable" under the Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act, with the 10-day-after-proof-of-loss regulation being the most concrete deadline.

## 6. SOL -- Breach of Contract

**6 years from date of breach.** Ga. Code Ann. 9-3-24; *Childs v. Armour Food Co.*, 175 Ga. App. 455, 333 S.E.2d 377 (1985).

- The period **can be shortened contractually**. It is undisputed that policies may require suit to be brought within a shorter period (commonly **1 year after the date of loss**). *Massey v. State Farm Fire & Cas. Co.*, 871 S.E.2d 685, 688 (Ga. Ct. App. 2022).

**CRITICAL:** Georgia's statutory SOL of 6 years is generous, but carriers routinely shorten it to 1-2 years by policy language. Always check the specific policy.

## 7. SOL -- Bad Faith

**Not addressed in guide -- web search required.**

The Merlin Guide references Ga. Code Ann. 33-4-6 (the bad faith penalty statute) but does not specify a standalone SOL for bad faith claims. Georgia bad faith under 33-4-6 is typically tied to the underlying contract claim SOL. Web search recommended to confirm the current limitation period and whether it runs independently.

## 8. Appraisal

- **Enforceable.** The guide states appraisal clauses are enforceable in Georgia.
- **Binding, absent fraud or mistake.** *McGowan v. Progressive Preferred Ins. Co.*, 618 S.E.2d 139 (Ga. Ct. App. 2005).
- **No general time frame** for invoking appraisal. Once invoked, look to the policy for the time requirement on appraiser appointment.
- Appraisal is to determine the **amount of damage**, not resolve questions of coverage or interpret provisions of the policy. *Lam v. Allstate Indem. Co.*, 755 S.E.2d 544 (Ga. Ct. App. 2014).
- **Participation in the appraisal process was a prerequisite to the insured's ability to file suit** against the property insurer. *Thornton v. Georgia Farm Bureau Mut. Ins. Co.*, 287 Ga. 379, 695 S.E.2d 642 (2010).
- Critical to read the appraisal clause in the specific policy for notice requirements, time frames, number of appraisers, and appraiser appointment requirements.

## 9. EUO (Examination Under Oath)

- Read the insurance policy -- many policies are now expanding the definition of who the policyholder must provide for EUO.
- **Books & Records:** Insurer has very broad discretion in what can be demanded.
- **Practical guidance:** Provide everything the insurance company asks for regarding books and records, or run the risk of having the claim denied.
- The guide does not elaborate on specific Georgia EUO case law or statutory requirements beyond the general policy-compliance advice.

## 10. Matching Regulation

**Not addressed in guide -- web search required.**

The Georgia Merlin Guide does not contain a section on matching statutes or regulations. Georgia does not appear to have a matching statute comparable to Florida's F.S. 626.9744 based on this guide.

## Notes

### Bad Faith Penalty Statute -- Ga. Code Ann. 33-4-6
This is Georgia's primary bad faith enforcement mechanism. Key features:
- Requires a **60-day pre-suit demand letter** to the insurer.
- Within **20 days of filing suit**, insured must also mail copies of the demand and complaint to the Commissioner of Insurance and the consumers' insurance advocate.
- The statute allows for penalty damages when the insurer fails to pay within 60 days of a demand and there was no reasonable cause for the delay.

### Proof of Loss Waiver -- Powerful Defense
Ga. Code Ann. 33-24-39 is a strategically important statute. If the carrier fails to provide a blank proof of loss form after receiving written request or written notice of loss, the carrier **waives the right to require proof of loss entirely**. This should be tracked and leveraged when carriers attempt to deny claims for failure to submit a formal proof of loss.

### Payment Timing
Georgia's 10-day payment deadline after proof of loss (Ga. Comp. R. and Regs. 120-2-52.03) is a regulatory standard, not a statutory penalty trigger like Texas's Prompt Payment Act. The enforcement mechanism runs through the Unfair Claims Settlement Practices framework under Ga. Code Ann. 33-6-34.

### Unfair Claims Settlement Practices
Ga. Code Ann. 33-6-34 requires insurers to:
- Make prompt offers when liability is reasonably clear
- Accept or deny claims within a reasonable period
- Not compel insureds to litigate by offering substantially less than amounts ultimately recovered

### Contractual SOL Warning
Georgia carriers commonly reduce the 6-year statutory SOL to 1 year by policy endorsement. *Massey v. State Farm* (2022) confirms this practice is enforceable. Always check the policy's suit limitation clause early in the claim lifecycle.

### Flood Insurance (Federal -- Not Georgia-Specific)
- Must file proof of loss within 60 days of damage (bar to recovery if missed).
- Proof of loss must be delivered, not mailed.
- Supplemental proofs of loss may be disallowed under flood policy.
- Suit must be filed within 1 year of disallowment of the claim. *Hairston v. Travelers Cas. & Sur. Co.*, 232 F.3d 1348 (11th Cir. 2000). But see *Kroll v. Johnson*, No. 14-2496, 2014 WL 4626009 (D.N.J. Sept. 15, 2014) (SOL runs one year from date of denial of proof of loss).

### Public Adjuster Commission Cap
Georgia caps PA commissions at **33.3%** of the insurance settlement. Ga. Code Ann. 33-23-43.3. However, if the insurer pays or commits to pay the policy limit within 72 hours of the loss being reported, the PA cannot charge a percentage -- only reasonable compensation for time and expenses.
