# Pennsylvania — State PRO Reference
**Source:** Merlin Law Group State Guide (Pennsylvania Edition, Version 4)
**Last verified:** 2026-03-04

## 1. Notice of Loss
**Immediate** notice required under the standard fire policy.
- **Citation:** 40 P.S. § 636 (Fire Policy)
- **Prejudice rule:** Failure to timely notify does **not** bar coverage unless the insurer proves it was prejudiced. *Brakeman v. Potomac Ins. Co.*, 472 Pa. 66 (1977).

## 2. Proof of Loss
**Look to the policy provision** — commonly 30 days, 60 days, or upon request.
- **Fire policy specific:** Proof of loss must be filed within **60 days** after the loss, unless time is extended in writing by the insurer. **40 P.S. § 636**
- Proof of loss is **not a condition precedent** unless the policy specifically requires it.
- If the insured has not substantially complied, the insurer must prove it suffered **prejudice**. *Brakeman v. Potomac Ins. Co.*, 472 Pa. 66 (1977).
- Insurer **can waive** the proof of loss requirement. *Cara v. Newark Fire Ins. Co.*, 312 Pa. 489 (1933).
- **Carrier form-furnishing obligation:** Upon notice of claim, the insurer must within **10 days** provide the claimant with forms, instructions, and reasonable assistance. **31 Pa. Code § 146.5(d)**

## 4. Carrier Acknowledgment Deadline
**10 working days** from receipt of a claim, the insurer must acknowledge receipt.
- **Citation:** 31 Pa. Code § 146.5 (implied by timeframe language in guide)
- Additionally, upon notice of claim, insurer must within **10 days** provide claimant with forms, instructions, and reasonable assistance. **31 Pa. Code § 146.5(d)**

## 5. Carrier Investigation/Payment Deadline
**15 calendar days** from receipt of a properly executed proof of loss, the insurer must **accept or deny** the claim.
- **Citation:** 31 Pa. Code § 146.7(a)(1)
- Insurer must give **written notice** to an unrepresented claimant at least **30 calendar days** before a statute of limitations or policy time limit may expire. **31 Pa. Code § 146.7(e)**

## 6. SOL — Breach of Contract
**4 years** from date of loss.
- **Citation:** 42 P.S. § 5525
- **However**, the standard fire insurance policy on property must contain a **one-year limitation provision** — no suit on the policy is sustainable unless commenced within **12 months** after inception of the loss. **40 P.S. § 636(2)**
- Contractual limitations period may be **shortened** if the period is reasonable. *Hahneman Univ. Hosp. v. Allshore*, 514 F.3d 300 (3rd Cir. 2008).
- Contractual limitations period may be **tolled** if a party's actions induce the claimant to forbear bringing suit. *Baker v. Pennsylvania Nat. Mut. Cas. Ins. Co.*, 370 Pa. Super. 461, 536 A.2d 1357 (1987).
- Contractual limitations period may be **waived** where the insurer's actions lead the insured to believe the limitation period will not be relied upon. *Jones v. Harleysville Mut. Ins. Co.*, 2006 PA Super 100, 900 A.2d 855 (2006).

## 7. SOL — Bad Faith
- **Statutory bad faith:** **2 years**
- **Common law bad faith** (breach of duty of good faith): **4 years**
- **Citation:** *Novinger v. Hartford Ins. Inc.*, 514 F.Supp.2d 662 (M.D. Pa. 2007)
- Limitation period commences when the cause of action accrues.

## 8. Appraisal
**Enforceable: Yes.** Appraisal clauses are enforceable in Pennsylvania.
- **Time limits to invoke:** Generally no time frame for invoking appraisal, but a party **cannot be prejudiced** by the timing of the demand. *Kester v. State Farm Fire & Cas. Co.*, 726 F. Supp. 1015 (E.D. Pa. 1989).
- Once invoked, look to the policy for time requirements on appraiser appointment.
- **Can be waived.**
- **Scope:** Appraisal is proper when parties disagree on the **extent of damage** or whether a covered peril caused the damage. Appraisal is **not proper** when the carrier claims an **exclusion** applies. *Currie v. State Farm Fire and Casualty Company*, 2014 U.S. Dist. Lexis 117970 (E.D. Pa. 2014).
- **Contingency fees for appraisers:** In the absence of contractual language requiring impartiality, a contingency fee arrangement does not, by itself, render the appraiser unfit. *Hozlock v. Donegal Companies/Donegal Mut. Ins. Co.*, 2000 PA Super 25, 745 A.2d 1261 (2000).
- **Challenging an appraiser:** To allege an appraiser is not disinterested, proof that partiality actually **clouded judgment and caused an unjust result** is required. *Hozlock*, 745 A.2d 1261 (2000).
- **Judicial review** of appraisal award is limited to fraud, misconduct, corruption, other irregularity causing an unjust result, and overstepping authority. *Riley v. Farmers Fire Ins. Co.*, 735 A.2d 124 (Sup. Ct. Pa. 1999).

## 9. EUO (Examination Under Oath)
The guide notes to **read the policy** because many policies are expanding the definition of who the policyholder must produce for EUO.
- **Books & Records:** Very broad discretion in what the insurer can demand. Practical guidance: provide everything the insurance company asks for, or risk having the claim denied.
- **Condition precedent to suit:** Not explicitly addressed in guide — web search required for case law on whether EUO compliance is a strict condition precedent in Pennsylvania.

## Notes

### Public Adjuster Contract Requirements (63 P.S. § 1605; 31 Pa. Code § 115.1 et seq.)
- Must be in writing, executed in duplicate.
- Insured has **right to cancel prior to midnight of the 4th calendar day** after execution.
- PA must notify insurer of representation within **2 calendar days** of entering the contract.
- Cannot collect fee when money is due but not paid; cannot collect entire fee from first check.
- Cannot require insured to authorize checks only in PA's name.
- Cannot impose late fees or collection costs.

### Settlement Communication Rules
- PA must communicate **all settlement offers** from insurer to the insured in writing within **5 business days**. 31 Pa. Code § 115.9(2).
- Must reply to written communications from insurer within **5 business days** (if response required). 31 Pa. Code § 115.9(1).

### Ethical Restrictions
- May **not** solicit business during the progress of a loss-producing occurrence (fire, tornado, storm, etc.) where immediate safety is paramount. 63 P.S. § 1606(a)(19).
- May **not** prohibit communication between insurer and insured. 63 P.S. § 1606(a)(20).
- May **not** act on personal injury or auto property damage claims. 63 P.S. § 1605(c).
- May **not** enter repair/restoration contracts with insured until verdict, award, or payment is received. 63 P.S. § 1605(d).
- Paying inducements to solicit PA business is a **3rd degree felony**. 63 P.S. § 1606(a)(6), § 1607.

### Flood Insurance (Federal — NFIP)
- Proof of loss must be filed within **60 days** of damage — failure is a **bar to recovery** (no prejudice rule). *Lucien v. U.S. Sec. Ins. Corp.*, 143 Fed. Appx 152 (11th Cir. 2005).
- Proof of loss must be **delivered, not mailed**.
- Suit must be filed within **1 year** of disallowment. *Hairston v. Travelers Cas. & Sur. Co.*, 232 F.3d 1348 (11th Cir. 2000).

### Strategic Considerations
- **Prejudice doctrine is strong in PA** — both for notice of loss and proof of loss failures, the burden shifts to the insurer to prove prejudice (*Brakeman*). This is favorable to the insured.
- **Watch for contractual SOL shortening** — standard fire policies require suit within 12 months (40 P.S. § 636(2)), which is much shorter than the 4-year statutory SOL. Track this carefully.
- **Tolling and waiver arguments available** — if the carrier's conduct induced delay, the contractual limitation period can be tolled (*Baker*) or waived (*Jones*).
- **Appraisal scope is limited** — carriers cannot force appraisal to resolve coverage/exclusion disputes, only damage amount disputes (*Currie*).
