# South Carolina — State PRO Reference
**Source:** Merlin Law Group State Guide (SC Edition)
**Last verified:** 2026-03-04

## 1. Notice of Loss
**Immediate.** Notice of a claim is a condition precedent to recovery on a policy. Where a notification provision is to be enforced, courts interpret "as soon as practicable" as meaning within a reasonable time frame.

**Citation:** *Vermont Mut. Ins. Co. v. Singleton*, 446 S.E.2d 417 (S.C. 1994)

## 2. Proof of Loss
**Look to policy provision** — typically 30 days, 60 days, or upon request.

**Carrier form-furnishing obligation:** When an insurer requires a written proof of loss after notice of loss, the insurer or its representative **must furnish a blank form** for that purpose. If forms are **not furnished within 20 days** after receipt of the notice of loss, the claimant is deemed to have complied with proof of loss requirements upon submitting written proof covering the occurrence, character, and extent of the loss within the time fixed in the policy.

**Citation:** S.C. Code Ann. section 38-59-10

## 6. SOL — Breach of Contract
**3 years** from the date the action accrues. Under the discovery rule, a breach of contract action accrues not on the date of the breach, but rather on the date the aggrieved party either discovered the breach, or could or should have discovered the breach through the exercise of reasonable diligence.

**Citations:**
- S.C. Code section 15-3-530(1)
- *Maher v. Tietex Corp.*, 500 S.E.2d 204, 207 (S.C. Ct. App. 1998)

## 7. SOL — Bad Faith
**3 years.** S.C. Code Ann. section 38-59-20 provides for bad faith actions. Bad faith claims are subject to the three-year statute of limitations. The limitations period accrues from the **date of the breach** (not the date of the loss).

**Citations:**
- S.C. Code Ann. section 38-59-20 (bad faith cause of action)
- S.C. Code section 15-3-530(5) (3-year SOL)
- *Lowcountry Block LLC v. Cincinnati Ins. Companies*, CV 9:17-1147-RMG, 2017 WL 3278878, at *2 (D.S.C. Aug. 1, 2017)

**Agent Negligence SOL:** 3 years after person knew or by exercise of reasonable diligence should have known of the cause of action. S.C. Code section 15-3-535.

## 8. Appraisal
**Enforceable: Yes.** Appraisal clauses are enforceable in South Carolina.

**Binding: Yes.**

**Time to invoke:** There is usually **no time frame** for invoking appraisal. Once invoked, look to the policy for time requirement on appraiser appointment.

**Condition precedent:** Participation in the appraisal process is a **condition precedent** to the insured's ability to file suit against the property insurer.

**Special rules:**
- A mere mistake of judgment by appraisers will not vitiate an award, but the award should be disregarded if appraisers are guilty of misconduct, bad faith, or unfairness substantially affecting the result.
- Appraisers are not agents, representatives, or advocates of the party by whom they are selected and should exercise judicial impartiality.
- Read the appraisal clause for notice requirements, time frames, number of appraisers, and appointment requirements.

**Citations:**
- *Miller v. British Am. Assur. Co.*, 238 S.C. 94, 102-03, 119 S.E.2d 527, 532 (1961) (condition precedent)
- *L.D. Jennings Co. v. N. River Ins. Co.*, 171 S.C. 548, 172 S.E. 700, 701 (1934) (impartiality standard)
- *Cleveland v. Insurance Company*, 150 S.C. 289 (mistake of judgment)
- *Fass v. Insurance Company*, 105 S.C. 364 (judicial impartiality)

## 9. EUO (Examination Under Oath)
**Not a condition precedent to suit**, but the insured's failure to cooperate may bar recovery under a policy **where the insurer can show prejudice** therefrom.

**Special rules:**
- Books and Records: Very broad discretion in what can be demanded by the insurer.
- Practical tip: Provide everything the insurance company asks for regarding books and records or risk having the claim denied.
- Many policies are expanding the definition of who the policyholder has to provide for EUO — read the policy carefully.

**Citation:** *Puckett v. State Farm General Ins. Co.*, 444 S.E.2d 523 (S.C. 1994)

## Notes

### Public Adjuster Licensing
- Governed by S.C. Code section 38-48-10 et seq.
- Must be licensed by the SC Department of Insurance. S.C. Code sections 38-48-20 through 38-48-50.
- Biennial fee of $80.

### Contract Requirements
- All PA contracts must be in writing. S.C. Code section 38-48-80.
- Insured has 5 business days to rescind contract (close of business on the 5th business day after signing). Waiver possible in writing for non-primary-residence properties. S.C. Code section 38-48-110.
- Must disclose: (1) hiring a PA is not required, (2) insured can communicate directly with insurer, (3) PA is not an agent/employee of the insurer.

### Ethical Standards (S.C. Code section 38-48-70 et seq.)
- PA may not offer coverage opinions.
- PA may not refer insured to contractors in which PA has financial interest.
- PA may not act as both contractor and PA on the same claim — this is a **felony** (up to 2 years imprisonment).
- Records must be maintained for 3 years after settlement/closing.
- Violations: Fine up to $2,500 (or $5,000 if willful), plus license suspension/revocation. S.C. Code sections 38-48-140, 38-2-10.

### Flood Insurance (Federal — applies in all states)
- Proof of loss: 60 days from damage. Failure is a bar to recovery.
- Must be delivered, not mailed.
- Suit must be filed within 1 year of disallowment.
- Supplemental proofs of loss may be disallowed.
