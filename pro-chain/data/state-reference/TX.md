# Texas -- State PRO Reference
**Source:** Merlin Law Group State Guide (Texas Edition, for Licensed Public Adjusters)
**Last verified:** 2026-03-04

## 1. Notice of Loss

**Deadline:** Immediate.

- Notice of a claim is a **condition precedent** to recovery on a policy. *American Teachers Life Ins. Co. v. Brugette*, 728 S.W.2d 763 (Tex. 1987).
- Policy provision requiring notice of loss to be given "as soon as practicable" is valid, and **failure to comply voids coverage**. *Broussard v. Lumbermens Mut. Cas. Co.*, 582 S.W.2d 261 (Tex. App. Beaumont 1979).

## 2. Proof of Loss

**Deadline:** Look to policy provision -- typically 30 days, 60 days, or upon request.

- Proof of loss is a **condition precedent** to recovery on a policy. *American Teachers Life Ins. Co. v. Brugette*, 728 S.W.2d 763 (Tex. 1987).
- To enforce a condition precedent to coverage, the insurer must prove that it was **prejudiced** by the insured's failure to comply with the condition. *Comsys Information Tech. Ser., Inc. v. Twin City Fire Ins. Co.*, 130 S.W.3d 181 (Tex. App.-Houston 2003).
- **Substantial compliance** with the requirement of furnishing a proof of loss is sufficient; however, it must occur within the time period allowed for the formal proof of loss. *Hanover Ins. Co. of New York v. Hagler*, 532 S.W.2d 136 (Tex. App. 1975).
- **Anti-technicality statute:** Tex. Ins. Code 705.003 protects an insured from an insurer's defense of fraud/misrepresentation. The statute can still apply even if a statement is not made in a formal proof of loss. *Temcharoen v. United Fire Lloyds*, 293 S.W.3d 332 (Tex. App.-Eastland 2009).
- **Carrier form-furnishing obligation:** Not explicitly addressed in this guide for non-flood claims. (Texas flood follows federal NFIP rules.)

## 3. Pre-Suit Notice Requirement + Cure Window

### Weather-Related Claims: Tex. Ins. Code 542A
- **60 days** prior to filing a lawsuit concerning a "force of nature" or "weather-related" event, a pre-suit notice letter must be sent to the insurer.
- The notice must detail:
  1. Acts or omissions giving rise to the claim
  2. Specific amount alleged to be owed by the insurer
  3. Amount of attorney fees incurred by the claimant
  4. All parties the policyholder intends to file suit against
- If pre-suit notice is **not provided, the lawsuit will be dismissed**.
- The notice letter **can be introduced into evidence by the insurer** -- it is crucial that the letter be accurate as to cost of repairs because it could later become the measurement of a jury verdict.
- Insurer can **request inspection of the property within 30 days** after receiving the notice letter.

### Bad Faith Notice: Tex. Ins. Code 541.154 and DTPA, Bus. & C. Code 17.505
- Must provide **written notice to the insurer at least 60 days before action is filed**, containing the specific complaint and amount of damages and expenses, including attorney's fees.
- Notice is **not required** if the action must be filed due to statute of limitations running.

## 4. Carrier Acknowledgment Deadline

**15 days** from receiving notice of loss.

In a first-party claim, the insurer must, within **15 days** of receiving notice of loss:
- Acknowledge receipt of the claim
- Commence an investigation
- Request all items, statements, and forms it believes will be required from the claimant

**Citation:** Tex. Ins. Code 542.051-542.061 (Prompt Payment of Claims Act).

## 5. Carrier Investigation / Payment Deadline

**Tex. Ins. Code 542.051-542.061 (Prompt Payment of Claims Act):**

- Within **15 business days** after receiving all items requested, insurer must notify the claimant in writing of acceptance or rejection of the claim.
- A notice of rejection must state the **reasons for the rejection**.
- If the insurer is unable to accept or reject within that period, it must notify the claimant of the reasons additional time is needed. The insurer must then accept or reject the claim **not later than the 45th day** after the date the insurer notifies the claimant it needs more time.
- If the insurer delays payment for a period exceeding the specified period **or for more than 60 days**, the insurer shall pay damages.
- If the claim is accepted, it must be **paid not later than the 5th business day** after notice to the claimant.

### Penalties for Violation
If the insurer violates the statutory time provisions, it is liable to pay the claimant:
- The **standard interest rate under the Finance Code plus 5%** on the amount of the claim as damages
- **Attorney's fees** as determined under Tex. Ins. Code 542A.007(a)

**Citation:** Tex. Ins. Code 542.060(c).

## 6. SOL -- Breach of Contract

**4 years** from the date the cause of action accrues. Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code Ann. 16.004.

- The limitations period **can be shortened by contract** but **cannot be reduced to less than 2 years**. Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. 16.070.
- If the limitations period is contractually shortened to 2 years, the contract **cannot establish a trigger for that period that occurs prior to the accrual of the cause of action** (because it would effectively shorten the limitation to less than 2 years). *Spicewood Summit Office Condominiums Ass'n, Inc. v. America First Lloyd's Ins. Co.*, 287 S.W.3d 461 (Tex. App.-Austin 2009).
- **Agent negligence:** 2 years.

## 7. SOL -- Bad Faith

**Same accrual rules as contract claims; 4-year statute applies.**

- A bad faith cause of action on an insurance claim **accrues on the date the insurer denies coverage**. *Murray v. San Jacinto Agency, Inc.*, 800 S.W.2d 826 (Tex. 1990).
- The same rule applies to Insurance Code and DTPA claims. *Stevens v. State Farm Fire & Cas. Co.*, 929 S.W.2d 665 (Tex. App.-Texarkana 1996).
- In a first-party action to determine whether coverage was properly denied, the agent negligence period begins to run from the date of denial. *Gailbreath v. White*, 903 S.W.2d 851 (Tex. App.-Texarkana 1995); but see *Sabine Towing & Transp. Co. v. Holliday Ins. Agency, Inc.*, 54 S.W.3d 57 (Tex. App.-Texarkana 2001).

## 8. Appraisal

- **Enforceable.** *In re Allstate County Mut. Ins. Co.*, 85 S.W.3d 193 (Tex. 2002).
- **Binding.**
- **No general time frame** for invoking appraisal. Once invoked, look to the policy for time requirement on appraiser appointment.
- **Can be waived.** Denying coverage under a policy waives the insurer's right to request appraisal. *In re Arcadia Ins. Co.*, 279 S.W.3d 777 (Tex. App.-Amarillo, 2007).
- **Condition precedent to filing suit** if the appraisal clause in the policy (which precludes suit until after the appraisal provision is complied with) is invoked. *Vanguard Underwriters Ins. Co v. Smith*, 999 S.W.2d 448 (Tex. App.-Amarillo, 1999).
- The Texas Supreme Court suggested that appraisers **can segregate covered and disputably covered items** with awards of values for each. *State Farm Lloyds v. Johnson*, 290 S.W.3d 886 (Tex. 2009).
- Critical to read the appraisal clause in the specific policy for notice requirements, time frames, number of appraisers, and appraiser appointment requirements.

## 9. EUO (Examination Under Oath)

- Read the insurance policy -- many policies are now expanding the definition of who the policyholder must provide for EUO.
- **Books & Records:** Insurer has very broad discretion in what can be demanded.
- **Practical guidance:** Provide everything the insurance company asks for regarding books and records, or run the risk of having the claim denied.
- The guide does not elaborate on specific Texas EUO case law or statutory requirements beyond the general policy-compliance advice.

## 10. Matching Regulation

The Texas Merlin Guide does not contain a section on matching statutes or regulations. Texas does not have a matching statute comparable to Florida's F.S. 626.9744.

## Notes

### Prompt Payment Act Penalties
The Texas Prompt Payment of Claims Act (Tex. Ins. Code 542.051-542.061) is one of the strongest carrier-deadline frameworks in the country. The penalty structure -- statutory interest rate + 5% + attorney fees -- creates real financial pressure on carriers to comply with timelines. Key dates to track:
- Day 0: Carrier receives notice of loss
- Day 15: Carrier must acknowledge, begin investigation, and request all needed items
- Day 15 (business days) after receiving all items: Accept or reject in writing
- Day 45 after extension notice: Final deadline if extension invoked
- Day 60: Hard outer limit before damages accrue
- Day 5 (business days) after acceptance: Payment must be made

### 542A Pre-Suit Notice as Evidence
The pre-suit notice letter under 542A can be introduced into evidence by the insurer. This means the repair cost and attorney fee figures in the notice letter could become the ceiling for a jury verdict. Accuracy is critical -- do not overstate or understate the claim amount.

### Anti-Technicality Statute
Tex. Ins. Code 705.003 is a powerful defense for insureds. It protects against insurer defenses based on fraud/misrepresentation technicalities in proofs of loss.

### Contractual SOL Shortening
Carriers commonly shorten the 4-year SOL to 2 years by contract. The 2-year floor is statutory (Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. 16.070). Any contractual trigger that starts the clock before accrual of the cause of action is void because it would effectively reduce the period below 2 years.

### Roofing Contractor Prohibition
A roofing contractor may not act as a public adjuster or advertise to adjust claims for any property for which the contractor is providing or may provide roofing services, even if the contractor is licensed as a public adjuster. Tex. Ins. Code 4102.163(a).

### Flood Insurance (Federal -- Not Texas-Specific)
- Must file proof of loss within 60 days of damage (bar to recovery if missed).
- Proof of loss must be delivered, not mailed.
- Supplemental proofs of loss may be disallowed under flood policy.
- Suit must be filed within 1 year of disallowment.
