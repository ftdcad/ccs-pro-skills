# Arizona — State PRO Reference
**Source:** Merlin Law Group State Guide
**Last verified:** 2026-03-04

## 1. Notice of Loss
**Deadline:** Per policy terms. No fixed statutory deadline — the policy language governs.

- If the policy expressly makes failure to give notice a ground of forfeiture, the plain language controls and there is generally no recovery when timely notice is a condition precedent. *Watson v. Ocean Acc. & Guarantee Corp.*, 238 P. 338, 340-41, 28 Ariz. 573, 579-80 (1925).
- If the policy does NOT expressly make failure to give notice a forfeiture, then failure merely postpones the time of bringing suit — the insured can still recover if notice is later served (provided the contractual limitations period has not expired). *Watson*, id.
- **Prejudice rule:** In the absence of prejudice, policy conditions requiring notice of loss or filing proof of loss within a specified time cannot work a forfeiture of the insured's claim. *Zuckerman v. Transamerica Ins. Co.*, 650 P.2d 441, 445, 133 Ariz. 139, 143 (1982).
- "Notification of claim" means any notification, whether in writing or other means acceptable under the policy, that reasonably apprises the insurer of the facts pertinent to a claim. Ariz. Admin. Code R20-6-801(B)(9).

## 2. Proof of Loss
**Deadline:** Per policy provision (commonly 30 days, 60 days, or upon request).

- An insurance company is not relieved of its contractual liability because an insured fails to submit a proof of loss, unless the company can show it was **prejudiced** by that failure. *Lindus v. Northern Ins. Co. of New York*, 438 P.2d 311, 315, 103 Ariz. 160, 164 (1968).
- The mere fact of delay is NOT a sufficient showing of prejudice. *Globe Indem. Co. v. Blomfield*, 562 P.2d 1372, 1374, 115 Ariz. 5, 7 (App. 1977).
- The proof-of-loss requirement is strongly construed against the carrier — substantial compliance is sufficient as a matter of law. *Truck Ins. Exchange v. Hale*, 386 P.2d 846, 849-850, 95 Ariz. 76, 81-83 (1963).
- **Carrier form-furnishing obligation:** Every insurer, upon receiving notification of a claim, shall promptly provide necessary claim forms, instructions, and reasonable assistance so that first-party claimants can comply with the policy conditions. Must be done within 10 working days of claim notification. Ariz. Admin. Code R20-6-801(E)(4).
- When the policy does not require specific proof of loss forms, there is no legal requirement that the insured use forms supplied by the insurer. *Maryland Cas. Co. v. Clements*, 487 P.2d 437, 442, 15 Ariz. App. 216, 221 (1971).

## 4. Carrier Acknowledgment Deadline
**10 working days.**

- Every insurer, upon receiving notification of a claim, shall within **10 working days** acknowledge receipt of the notice, unless payment is made within that period. If acknowledgment is by means other than writing, an appropriate notation shall be made in the claim file and dated. Ariz. Admin. Code R20-6-801(E)(1).
- Notification given to an agent of an insurer constitutes notification to the insurer. Ariz. Admin. Code R20-6-801(E)(1).
- An appropriate reply shall be made within **10 working days** on all other pertinent communications from a claimant that reasonably suggest a response is expected. Ariz. Admin. Code R20-6-801(E)(3).

## 5. Carrier Investigation/Payment Deadline
**Investigation: 30 days. Accept/Deny decision: 15 working days after receipt of proof of loss. Ongoing updates every 45 days.**

- Every insurer shall complete investigation of a claim within **30 days** after notification of claim, unless such investigation cannot reasonably be completed within that time. Ariz. Admin. Code R20-6-801(F).
- Within **15 working days** after receipt of properly executed proofs of loss, the first-party claimant shall be advised of acceptance or denial. Denial must be in writing, must reference the specific policy provision/condition/exclusion relied upon, and a copy must be kept in the claim file. Ariz. Admin. Code R20-6-801(G)(1)(a).
- If more time is needed: the insurer must notify the claimant within 15 working days after receipt of proof of loss, giving reasons. If investigation remains incomplete, the insurer shall send a letter every **45 days** from initial notification explaining why additional time is needed. Ariz. Admin. Code R20-6-801(G)(1)(b).
- **Interest accrual:** Payment should be made within **30 days** after receipt of a proper proof of loss, or interest begins to accrue. Arizona DOI Circular Letter No. 2000-4; Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. section 20-462.

## 6. SOL — Breach of Contract
**6 years (written contract), but policy may shorten to no less than 1 year.**

- Contract claims based on a written contract are subject to a **six-year** statute of limitations. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. section 12-548.
- However, the cause of action accrues from the date of the event, and an insurer may set a limitations period as short as **one year** from the date of the event resulting in the loss. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. section 20-1115.
- A policy may require suit within a shorter period than six years, so long as the limitations period is not shortened to less than one year. *Nangle v. Farmers Ins. Co. of Arizona*, 73 P.3d 1252 (Ariz. Ct. App. 2003).

## 7. SOL — Bad Faith
**2 years (tort statute of limitations).**

- Bad faith claims are governed by the tort statute of limitations: **two years** after the cause of action accrues. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. section 12-542.
- A first-party bad faith claim accrues when the insurance company intentionally denies, fails to process, or fails to pay a claim without a reasonable basis. *Uyleman v. D.S. Rentco*, 981 P.2d 1081, 1084, 194 Ariz. 300, 303 (1999).
- If an insurer acts unreasonably in the manner in which it processes a claim, it will be held liable for bad faith **without regard to its ultimate merits**. *Zilisch v. State Farm Mut. Auto. Ins. Co.*, 995 P.2d 276, 196 Ariz. 234 (2000).

## 8. Appraisal
**Enforceable. Binding (per standard policy language). Right generally waived if not requested within 1 year of loss.**

- The function of appraisers is to determine the amount of damage — NOT to resolve coverage questions or interpret policy provisions. *Hanson v. Commercial Union Ins. Co.*, 723 P.2d 101, 104, 150 Ariz. 283, 286 (App. 1986).
- The right to request appraisal is generally waived if not requested within **one year** after the loss. *Meineke v. Twin City Fire Ins. Co.*, 892 P.2d 1365, 1371, 181 Ariz. 576, 582 (App. 1994).
- Public adjusters may represent insureds in informal, umpired appraisal proceedings conducted pursuant to the insurance contract where the sole issue is documentation and valuation of loss. Arizona DOI Circular Letter 2000-11 (September 20, 2000).

## 9. EUO (Examination Under Oath)
**Cooperation is broadly required. No specific EUO statute cited in guide — governed by policy terms.**

- Many policies are expanding the definition of who the policyholder must provide for EUO — read the specific policy.
- Books and records: Very broad discretion in what the insurer can demand.
- **Practical rule:** Provide everything the insurance company asks for regarding books and records, or risk having the claim denied.

## Notes

### Unfair Claim Settlement Practices — No Private Right of Action
Arizona has adopted unfair claims settlement practices statutes (A.R.S. section 20-461) and regulations (Ariz. Admin. Code R20-6-801), but the act **does not provide any private right or cause of action** to or on behalf of any insured. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. section 20-461(D). This is significant — violations are enforceable by the DOI, not directly by the insured in court.

### Key Unfair Claims Practices (A.R.S. section 20-461)
The statute prohibits conduct committed with a frequency indicating a general business practice, including:
- Misrepresenting pertinent facts or policy provisions (section 20-461(A)(1))
- Failing to acknowledge and act promptly on claims (section 20-461(A)(2))
- Failing to adopt reasonable investigation standards (section 20-461(A)(3))
- Refusing to pay without reasonable investigation (section 20-461(A)(4))
- Failing to affirm or deny coverage within reasonable time after proof of loss (section 20-461(A)(5))
- Not attempting good faith prompt/fair settlements when liability is reasonably clear (section 20-461(A)(6))
- Failing to recognize valid assignment of a claim (section 20-461(A)(7))
- Compelling insureds to litigate by offering substantially less than amounts ultimately recovered (section 20-461(A)(8))

### Assignment of Claims
An insurer must recognize a valid assignment of a claim. The insurer retains rights to notice of loss and all policy defenses, but cannot otherwise restrict assignment after loss occurs. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. section 20-461(A)(7).

### Agent Negligence SOL
Two years (tort statute). Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. section 12-542. Insurance agents owe a duty of reasonable care as advisors with special knowledge. *Webb v. Gittlen*, 174 P.3d 275, 279, 217 Ariz. 363, 367 (2008).

### Flood Insurance (Federal — Applies in All States)
- Proof of loss: 60 days from loss. 44 C.F.R. Pt. 61, App. A(1). Failure is a bar to recovery.
- Suit: Within 1 year of written denial.
- NFIP terms cannot be waived by WYO insurer without written consent of Federal Insurance Administrator. 44 C.F.R. sections 61.4(b), 61.13(d)-(e), 62.23(c)-(d).
