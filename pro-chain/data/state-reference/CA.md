# California — State PRO Reference
**Source:** Merlin Law Group State Guide
**Last verified:** 2026-03-04

## 1. Notice of Loss
**Deadline:** Immediate. Condition precedent to recovery.

- Notice of a claim is a condition precedent to recovery on a policy. *1231 Euclid Homeowners Assn. v. State Farm*, (2006) 135 Cal. App. 4th 1008.
- **Prejudice rule:** Delay in giving notice is not fatal to recovery unless the insurer is **substantially prejudiced**. *Shell Oil Co. v. Winterthur Swiss Ins. Co.*, (1993) 12 Cal. App. 4th 715.
- Public adjusters must notify the insurer, its adjuster, or its attorney within 3 business days after the rescission/cancellation period has expired that they have entered into a contract with the insured. Cal. Ins. Code section 15027(w).

## 2. Proof of Loss
**Deadline:** Per policy provision (commonly 30 days, 60 days, or upon request).

- Proof of loss is a condition precedent to recovery on a policy. *1231 Euclid Homeowners Assn. v. State Farm*, (2006) 135 Cal. App. 4th 1008.
- **Prejudice rule:** Delay in giving proof of loss is not fatal to recovery unless insurer is substantially prejudiced. *Shell Oil Co. v. Winterthur Swiss Ins. Co.*, (1993) 12 Cal. App. 4th 715.
- **Carrier form-furnishing obligation:** Not specifically addressed in guide — web search required. (Note: California Fair Claims Settlement Practices Regulations, 10 CCR section 2695.5(e), likely governs — verify independently.)

## 5. Carrier Investigation/Payment Deadline
**Payment: 30 days after loss amount agreed upon or settled (fire insurance). Interest accrues at 10% per annum if late.**

- Implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing requires prompt payment. An insurer that unreasonably delays or fails to pay benefits due under the policy may be held liable in tort for breach of the implied covenant. *Rappaport-Scott v. Interinsurance Exchange of the Automobile Club*, (2007) 146 Cal. App. 4th 831.
- **Fire insurance:** Payment must be made within **30 days** after the amount of loss has been agreed upon or settled. Cal. Ins. Code section 2057.
- Interest on late payment accrues at the prevailing legal rate for breach of contract: **10% per annum**. Cal. Ins. Code section 3289(b).
- **Declared state of emergency:** Insured has **36 months** from the date of first ACV payment to collect full replacement cost. Insured's time to make a replacement cost claim must be extended for an additional 6 months if good cause is shown. Cal. Ins. Code section 2051.5(b)(1)(B).
- (Note: Cal. Ins. Code section 790.03 and 10 CCR section 2695.7 set additional investigation/acceptance/denial timeframes — not covered in guide. Web search required for 40-day acceptance/denial rule.)

## 6. SOL — Breach of Contract
**Written contract: 4 years general. Standard fire policy: 1 year from date of loss (2 years if declared state of emergency). Policy can contractually shorten.**

- **General written contract SOL:** 4 years.
- **Standard Fire Policy:** Limitation period is **one year** from date of loss. Cal. Ins. Code section 2071.
- **Declared state of emergency:** Limitation period is **two years** from date of loss. Cal. Ins. Code section 2071.
- Other policies: Period can be shortened contractually. Policy provisions limiting time to sue are valid if clearly stated and not "so unreasonable as to show imposition or undue advantage." *Fageol v. Pacific Indemnity Co.*, (1941) 18 Cal.2d 748.
- Homeowner policies typically limit time to sue to one or two years from date of loss.
- **Tolling:** SOL is tolled from the time the insured gives notice until coverage is formally denied in writing. *Prudential-LMI Com. Ins. v. Superior Court*, 798 P.2d 1230 (1990).
- Equitable tolling also applies to first-party commercial all-risk policies. *San Jose Crane v. Lexington Ins. Co.*, (1991) 227 Cal. App. 3d 1314.
- However, limitations period is NOT tolled in an agent negligence action while the insurer considers the insured's claim. *Hydro-Mill Co., Inc. v. Hayword, Tilton & Rolapp Ins. Assoc.*, (2004) 115 Cal. App. 4th 1145.
- Insurer need not show prejudice from the insured's delay in filing suit.

## 8. Appraisal
**Enforceable. Binding. Usually no time frame for invocation. Cannot be compelled during government-declared disaster.**

- Appraisal determines amount of damage — not coverage questions or policy interpretation. *Hughes v. Potomac Ins. Co.*, 18 Cal. Rptr. 650 (Cal. App. 1962).
- Appraisal provision in policy is treated as an agreement to arbitrate under the California Arbitration Act, Code Civ. Pro. section 1280(a).
- **Time to invoke:** Usually no time frame for invoking appraisal. Once invoked, look to policy for time requirement on appraiser appointment.
- **Waiver:** Appraisal can be waived. *Silver v. California Fair Plan Ass'n*, 2002 WL 1293003 (Cal. App. 2002) (insurer waived by engaging in extensive discovery on amount of loss and claims/defenses).
- Appraisal clauses are enforceable. Code Civ. Pro. section 1281.
- **Government-declared disaster exception:** Appraisal "may not be compelled" under standard fire policy, standard residential property insurance, and basic residential earthquake policies during declared disasters. Cal. Ins. Code sections 2071(a); 790.031; 10082.3; 10087.
- **Voluntary mediation program** exists for disputes arising from residential property insurance losses due to fire or earthquake when the Governor has declared a state of emergency. Cal. Ins. Code section 10089.70; section 2696.1 et seq.
- Critical to read the specific appraisal clause in the policy for notice requirements, time frames, number of appraisers, and appointment requirements.

## 9. EUO (Examination Under Oath)
**Cooperation required per policy terms. No specific EUO statute cited in guide.**

- Many policies are expanding the definition of who the policyholder must provide for EUO — read the specific policy.
- Books and records: Very broad discretion in what the insurer can demand.
- **Practical rule:** Provide everything the insurance company asks for regarding books and records, or risk having the claim denied.

## Notes

### Prompt Payment — Tort Liability for Unreasonable Delay
California recognizes the implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing. An insurer that unreasonably delays or fails to pay benefits due is liable **in tort** for breach of the implied covenant. *Rappaport-Scott v. Interinsurance Exchange of the Automobile Club*, (2007) 146 Cal. App. 4th 831. This is significant because tort damages can include emotional distress and punitive damages, unlike contract damages.

### SOL Tolling — Major Strategic Advantage
The SOL is tolled from the time the insured gives notice of the claim until coverage is formally denied **in writing**. *Prudential-LMI*, supra. This means a carrier that never formally denies in writing may never start the clock. This is a powerful tool.

### State of Emergency — Special Rules
California has several special protections during declared emergencies:
- **Contents coverage advance:** 30% of contents coverage owed (up to $250,000) without an itemized claim. Cal. Ins. Code section 10103.7(b)(1).
- **ALE advance:** If total loss, insurer must advance no less than 4 months of living expenses. Cal. Ins. Code section 2061(a)(1).
- **Replacement cost extension:** 36 months from first ACV payment + 6-month extension for good cause. Cal. Ins. Code section 2051.5(b)(1)(B).
- **SOL extension:** 2 years instead of 1 year under standard fire policy. Cal. Ins. Code section 2071.
- **Appraisal cannot be compelled.** Cal. Ins. Code sections 2071(a); 790.031; 10082.3; 10087.
- **Public adjuster cancellation:** 5 calendar days (vs. normal 3 business days) if loss is in area subject to catastrophic disaster.

### Public Adjuster Rescission Weapon
If a public adjuster misrepresents or conceals a material fact from the insured prior to contract execution, the insured is entitled to rescind the contract **without time limit**. Cal. Ins. Code section 15027. The guide warns this can be abused.

### Flood Insurance (Federal — Applies in All States)
- Proof of loss: 60 days from damage. Failure is a bar to recovery. Must be delivered, not mailed.
- Suit: Within 1 year of disallowment/denial.
- Supplemental proofs of loss after additional damage discovered may be disallowed.
