# Louisiana — State PRO Reference
**Source:** Merlin Law Group State Guide (Louisiana Edition, Version 2)
**Last verified:** 2026-03-04

## 1. Notice of Loss
**Timely or as required by policy.** An insurer cannot avoid liability under the policy for untimely notice **unless the insurer is actually prejudiced** by the insured's failure to give timely notice. If the insurer receives sufficient information to act on the claim, the manner in which it obtained the information is immaterial.

**Exception:** If the policy includes a notice provision and compliance is a **condition precedent**, and the insured fails to comply, the claim will be denied **without** the need for the insurer to demonstrate prejudice.

**Citations:**
- *State v. Nat'l Union Fire Ins. Co.*, 56 So. 3d 1236 (La. Ct. App. 2011) (prejudice required)
- *Auster Oil & Gas, Inc. v. Stream*, 891 F.2d 570 (5th Cir. 1990) (condition precedent exception)

## 2. Proof of Loss
**Look to policy provision** — typically 30 days, 60 days, or upon request.

Louisiana follows the **substantial compliance** rule — the insured is required only to demonstrate substantial compliance with the insurer's request for proof of loss. Strict compliance with contractual proof of loss provisions **can void coverage only if the insurer proves it was actually prejudiced** by the lapse.

**Carrier form-furnishing obligation:** Not specifically addressed in guide — web search required.

**Citations:**
- *Sevier v. USF&G Co.*, 497 So.2d 1380 (La. 1986) (substantial compliance)
- *Kiln Underwriting, Ltd v. Jesuit H. Sch. of New Orleans*, 06-4350 (E.D. La. Oct. 24, 2008) (prejudice required for strict compliance)

## 6. SOL — Breach of Contract
**2 years from the date of loss** (Louisiana uses the term "prescriptive period" rather than "statute of limitations").

**Key rules:**
- Contractual shortening of the prescriptive period to **less than 2 years is not allowed**.
- Prescription begins to run from the **date of the loss** (not date of denial or discovery).

**Citations:**
- La. Rev. Stat. section 22:868 (no shortening below 2 years)
- La. C.C. art. 3454 (runs from date of loss)

## 7. SOL — Bad Faith
**10 years** from the breach of the duty of good faith and fair dealing.

**Citation:** Per guide — likely based on La. C.C. art. 3499 (10-year prescriptive period for personal actions). Specific statute not cited in guide.

## 8. Appraisal
**Enforceable: Yes.** Appraisal clauses are enforceable in Louisiana.

**Binding: Yes.**

**Can be waived:** Yes.

**Time to invoke:** There is usually **no time frame** for invoking appraisal. Once invoked, look to policy for time requirement on appraiser appointment.

**Condition precedent to appraisal:** Insureds cannot demand appraisal until they have **complied with all post-loss policy provisions**, including notice of loss, EUO, and providing all reasonable information requested by the insurer.

**Special rules:**
- Causation may be analyzed by appraisers, but coverage disputes are reserved for courts.
- **Appraisers must be registered** with the Louisiana Department of Insurance (appraiser registration form at ldi.la.gov).
- A **public adjuster cannot act as an appraiser or umpire** on any claim the PA is adjusting or has adjusted. La. Stat. section 22:1706.
- Read the appraisal clause for notice requirements, time frames, number of appraisers, and appointment requirements.

**Citations:**
- *Dufrene v. Certain Interested Underwriters at Lloyd's of London*, 91 So.3d 397 (La. App. 2012) (enforceable)
- *Triple K, Inc. v. Century Sur. Co.*, 2010 WL 3418237 (E.D. La. Aug. 23, 2010) (can be waived)
- *St. Charles Parish Hosp. Service Dist. No. 1 v. United Fire and Cas. Co.*, 681 F.Supp.2d 748 (E.D. La. 2010) (causation vs. coverage)
- La. Stat. section 22:1706 (PA cannot serve as appraiser)
- R.S. 22:1311 (appraisal provisions)

## 9. EUO (Examination Under Oath)
The guide states to **read the insurance policy** because many policies are expanding the definition of who the policyholder has to provide for EUO.

**Books and Records:** Very broad discretion in what can be demanded by the insurer. Practical tip: Provide everything the insurance company asks for regarding books and records or run the risk of having the claim denied.

## Notes

### Louisiana Act No. 345 — Depreciation and O&P Rules (R.S. section 22:1892)
**(Effective August 1, 2021)** — This is critically important for claims handling:

**Depreciation:**
- "Depreciation" includes cost of goods, materials, **labor**, and services necessary to replace, repair, or rebuild. R.S. 22:1892(B)(6)(a)(ii)
- A policy covering damaged property **may** allow for depreciation. R.S. 22:1892(B)(6)(b)
- Policy **must provide notice** that depreciation may be deducted/withheld, on a form approved by the commissioner. R.S. 22:1892(B)(6)(c)
- If depreciation is applied, insurer **must provide a written explanation** of how it was calculated. R.S. 22:1892(B)(6)(d)
- Depreciation must be **reasonable** and based on a combination of objective criteria and subjective assessment, including actual condition of property prior to loss. R.S. 22:1892(B)(6)(e)

**Overhead and Profit (O&P):**
- Insurer **must include general contractor's overhead and profit** in payments when GC services are reasonably foreseeable. Applies to both replacement cost and actual cash value policies. R.S. 22:1892(F)(1)
- **Deduction of prospective contractor O&P and sales tax in determining ACV is NOT allowed** — on either replacement cost or ACV policies. R.S. 22:1892(F)(2)

**Preferred Vendor Prohibition:**
- An insurer **cannot require** that repairs or replacement be by a particular preferred vendor or recommended contractor on residential or commercial property damage claims. R.S. 22:1892(E)(1)

### PA Fee Structure
- Fee **may not be contingent upon, or calculated as a percentage of**, the amount of any claim or claims paid. La. Stat. section 22:1703.
- This is a significant departure from most states — Louisiana PAs must charge flat or hourly fees, not contingency percentages.

### Contract Requirements
- 3 business day cancellation window. La. Rev. Stat. section 22:1704.
- PA must maintain contract copies for at least 5 years. La. Stat. section 22:1705.

### Ethical Standards (L.S. 22:1706)
- PA cannot act as company adjuster or independent adjuster.
- Cannot solicit during progress of a loss-producing occurrence.
- Cannot solicit employment for attorneys, contractors, or subcontractors (but can recommend — no compensation for referral).
- Cannot acquire interest in salvage.
- Violations are a **felony** — up to 5 years imprisonment and $5,000 fine.

### Flood Insurance (Federal — applies in all states)
- Proof of loss: 60 days from damage. Failure is a bar to recovery.
- Must be delivered, not mailed.
- Suit must be filed within 1 year of disallowment.
