# North Carolina — State PRO Reference
**Source:** Merlin Law Group State Guide
**Last verified:** 2026-03-04

## 1. Notice of Loss
**Deadline:** Immediate.
Notice of a claim is a condition precedent to recovery on a policy. However, delay in giving notice is not fatal to recovery unless the insurer is substantially harmed.
**Citation:** N.C. Gen. Stat. § 58-44-50

## 2. Proof of Loss
**Deadline:** Look to individual policy provision — commonly 30 days, 60 days, or upon request.
Proof of loss is a condition precedent to recovery on a policy **if** the failure to provide it materially prejudices the insurer. *Smith v. N. Carolina Farm Bureau Mut. Ins. Co.*, 321 N.C. 60, 361 S.E.2d 571 (1987).

**Carrier form-furnishing obligation:** Insurer must furnish the insured a blank proof of loss form within **15 days** after notice of loss. If the insurer fails to furnish the form within 15 days, the insured is **deemed to have complied** with the proof of loss requirement.
**Citation:** N.C. Gen. Stat. § 58-3-40

## 6. SOL — Breach of Contract
**3 years** from the date of breach.
The phrase "inception of loss," as used in a fire policy, means the policy limitation period runs from the date of the occurrence of the event out of which the claim for recovery arose. *Marshburn v. Associated Indem. Corp.*, 353 S.E.2d 123 (N.C. Ct. App. 1987).
**Citation:** N.C. Gen. Stat. § 1-52; *Penley v. Penley*, 332 S.E.2d 51 (N.C. 1985)

## 7. SOL — Bad Faith
**3 years** (tort claims statute of limitations). The period begins to run when the plaintiff's right to maintain an action for the wrong alleged accrues.
**Citation:** N.C. Gen. Stat. Ann. § 1-52; *Housecalls Home Health Care, Inc. v. State, Dept. of Health & Human Services*, 682 S.E.2d 741 (N.C. Ct. App. 2009), writ denied, review denied, 363 N.C. 802, 690 S.E.2d 697 (2010)

**Also noted — Agent Negligence:** 3 years. N.C. Gen. Stat. § 1-52.

## 8. Appraisal
**Enforceable:** Yes. Appraisal clauses are enforceable in North Carolina.
**Binding:** Yes.
**Condition precedent:** Participation in the appraisal process is a **condition precedent** to the insured's ability to file suit against the property insurer. *Patel v. Scottsdale Ins. Co.*, 728 S.E.2d 394 (N.C. App. 2012).
**Time limits:** There is usually **no time frame** for invoking appraisal. Once invoked, look to the policy for time requirements on appraiser appointment.
**Scope:** Appraisal determines the **amount of damage only** — it does not resolve questions of coverage or interpret provisions of the policy. *North Carolina Farm Bureau Mut. Ins. Co. v. Sadler*, 711 S.E.2d 114 (N.C. 2011).
**Practice note:** Read the appraisal clause in the policy carefully for notice requirements, time frames, number of appraisers, and any requirements for the appointment of appraisers.

## 9. EUO (Examination Under Oath)
The guide does not state whether EUO is a condition precedent to suit in North Carolina. It provides practical guidance only:
- Read the insurance policy thoroughly; many policies now expand the definition of who the policyholder must provide for EUO.
- Books & Records: Insurer has very broad discretion in what it can demand.
- **Practical tip from Merlin:** Provide everything the insurance company asks for regarding books and records, or risk having the claim denied.

## Notes

### Public Adjuster Contract Requirements (N.C. Gen. Stat. § 58-33A-65)
- All PA contracts must be in writing, executed in duplicate.
- Contract must include: PA name/address/license number, insured info, description of services, signatures, attestation of bond, full fee/commission disclosure.
- If compensation is percentage-based, the exact percentage must be specified.
- PA may be named as co-payee on insurer's payment.
- **Insured rescission right:** 3 business days after signing. Anything of value returned within 15 business days of cancellation notice.

### 72-Hour Carrier Payment Rule (N.C. Gen. Stat. § 58-33A-65)
If the insurer, within **72 hours** of the loss being reported, either pays or commits in writing to pay the policy limit, the public adjuster:
- Cannot receive a percentage-based commission.
- Must inform the insured that the loss recovery amount might not be increased.
- Is entitled only to reasonable hourly compensation for time spent and expenses incurred.

### Catastrophic Incident Fee Cap (N.C. Gen. Stat. § 58-33A-60(d))
During a declared catastrophic incident, no PA may charge more than **10%** of any insurance settlement or proceeds. No upfront fees, retainers, or deposits are allowed before settlement.

### Flood Insurance (Federal — NFIP)
- Proof of loss must be filed within **60 days** of damage (failure is a bar to recovery).
- Proof of loss must be **delivered**, not mailed.
- Suit must be filed within **1 year** of disallowment of the claim.
- Only express written consent of the Federal Insurance Administrator or FEMA guidelines can waive the proof of loss requirement.

### Solicitation Restrictions
- PA cannot solicit during a loss-producing occurrence in progress. N.C. Gen. Stat. § 58-33A-80(b).
- No solicitation between 9 PM and 9 AM. N.C. Gen. Stat. § 58-33A-80(k).
- PA may not agree to any loss settlement without the insured's knowledge and consent. N.C. Gen. Stat. § 58-33A-80(j).
